## Overview

A major thread of my research examines how anthropogenic activities — whale watching, vessel traffic, and fisheries entanglement — affect cetacean behaviour and energetic condition. This work is explicitly designed to bridge the gap between research and policy, and several studies have directly informed US federal regulation and Australian government management decisions.

## Whale watching and swim-with-whale tourism

I have conducted systematic research on commercial whale watching and swim-with-whale tourism operations in Hawaii, Australia, Japan, and other locations. My studies quantify how the presence of vessels and swimmers alters humpback whale behaviour, including changes in surface activity, travel rates, and time spent in different behavioural states.

## Vessel interactions and entanglement

Using drone-based imagery, I have quantified rates of non-lethal scarring from vessel strikes and entanglement in the east Australian humpback whale population. I am also a certified NOAA Level 3 Large Whale Entanglement Responder and have assisted in numerous entanglement response events in Hawaii.

## Policy outcomes

- Invited subject matter expert, Australian federal government Biologically Important Areas determination for humpback whales (2024–2025)
- Written submission to NOAA Fisheries informing time-area closures to protect spinner dolphins under the Marine Mammal Protection Act (2021)
- Written submission to NOAA Fisheries supporting spinner dolphin management action (2016)
- Written submission supporting humpback whale downlisting in Australia (2021)
- Invited Participant, IWC Scientific Committee (2018–present)
